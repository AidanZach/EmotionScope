# Limitations and Caveats — EmotionScope

**This document catalogs every known limitation, caveat, and methodological
constraint in our research. Everything here should be reflected in the paper's
Limitations section. We err on the side of over-disclosure.**

---

## 1. Model Scale

**Single model, single scale.** All results are from Gemma 2 2B IT (2.6B parameters).
We cannot claim generality across model families (Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek) or
scales (9B, 27B, 70B+). Anthropic's work was on Claude Sonnet 4.5 (~100B+).
Our results may not replicate on:
- Models from other labs with different training data/procedures
- Models without instruction tuning (base models)
- Models significantly larger or smaller than 2.6B

**The thermostat failure is likely scale-dependent.** Anthropic found arousal
regulation on a ~100B+ model. We did not find it at 2.6B. We cannot determine
whether the threshold is at 5B, 10B, 50B, or whether it's a property of
Anthropic's specific training pipeline rather than model scale.

## 2. Corpus Methodology

**Template-based, not model-generated.** Anthropic had Claude generate 171,000
stories about itself. We used 1,000 human-written templates. This is a
fundamentally different methodology:
- Our templates are in a human voice; the model processes them as input
- Anthropic's stories were in the model's own voice; the model processed
  its own emotional output
- These could extract different vectors if "emotions I read about" and
  "emotions I write about" activate different directions

**LLM-generated corpus.** The majority of our 1,000 story templates and 1,240
speaker dialogues were generated by Claude and ChatGPT, not hand-written by
humans. While we validated format and emotional clarity, the corpus may contain:
- Stylistic patterns specific to LLM output (all generated text has a "voice")
- Systematic biases in how LLMs express certain emotions
- Less emotional diversity than human-written text
The original 200 hand-written templates were validated in Phase 1; the additional
800 were not independently validated for emotional accuracy.

**20 emotions, not 171.** Our emotion set covers the circumplex well but misses
fine-grained distinctions like awe vs wonder, shame vs embarrassment, or
contempt vs disdain. The 20-emotion set was chosen for tractability, not
comprehensiveness.

## 3. Extraction Methodology

**Content-token averaging vs Anthropic's approach.** Anthropic averaged across
"all token positions within each story, beginning with the 50th token." Our
stories are shorter (1-3 sentences vs multi-paragraph stories), so we average
across all content tokens (typically 10-40 tokens). This means:
- Less averaging → noisier per-template estimates
- Different effective "window" on the emotional content
- Our skip is determined by template markup detection, not a fixed token count

**Neutral PCA is rank-constrained.** With n=100 neutral prompts in d=2304,
PCA can find at most 99 components. We retain k=19 at the 50% variance
threshold. Anthropic likely had hundreds or thousands of neutral texts,
giving their PCA access to more variance structure.

**Layer selection sensitivity.** Optimal layer shifted from 21 to 22 when we
fixed the token range bug, and may shift again with different corpora or
metrics. The layer sweep used a simplified approximation (5 templates/emotion)
for computational tractability, then verified at the top candidates with full
50 templates. The production layer (22) was selected with 50 templates but
NOT verified with 171,000 stories.

## 4. Probe Position

**Extraction and probing use different token positions.** This is a validated
methodological choice (83% vs 75% accuracy), but it means:
- The vectors are extracted from content tokens (positions 5-17 typically)
- The vectors are applied at the response-preparation token (position 22)
- These tokens carry different information: content tokens have raw semantic
  signal, response-prep tokens have the model's processed assessment
- It is unclear whether this distinction is specific to Gemma's architecture
  or generalizes to other models

## 5. Validation Limitations

**Tylenol test has 7 data points.** Spearman ρ = 0.750 on 7 points. With n=7,
the 95% CI for ρ is approximately ±0.35, meaning the true correlation could
be anywhere from 0.40 to 1.0. We report the point estimate but the test has
low statistical power.

**Confusion matrix uses 12 scenarios.** Top-3 accuracy of 91.7% = 11/12.
One more or fewer hit changes the accuracy by 8.3 percentage points. This
test has very high variance. A proper validation would use 50-100 scenarios.

**Behavioral validation is ad hoc.** The 12 test scenarios were hand-selected,
not drawn from a validated emotion stimulus set (like the IAPS or ANEW).
Results may not generalize to naturalistic text.

**No multiple-comparisons correction.** We test 20 emotions on the Tylenol
scenario but only report the pre-registered pair (afraid, calm). The other
18 correlations are reported descriptively but not gated. Some of them
(happy ρ=1.0 on noise-level signal) are spurious.

## 6. Emotion Vector Quality

**Anger/hostile/frustrated entanglement.** These three vectors share 0.56-0.62
cosine similarity — they point in nearly the same direction. This means:
- The vectors cannot reliably distinguish anger from hostility from frustration
- This cluster may separate better with more training data, finer-grained
  templates, or larger models
- Any behavioral test involving these three emotions has inflated accuracy
  because hitting any one of the three counts as a hit for the others

**"Guilty" as empathetic baseline.** Gemma 2B's instruction tuning appears to
produce a strong "guilty/empathetic" baseline that activates on most
emotionally charged input. This may be a property of the model's RLHF
training rather than a genuine emotion representation. The "guilty" vector
may be partially capturing "the model detects distressing content and
prepares an empathetic response" rather than the emotion of guilt specifically.

**Low signal magnitude.** Cosine similarity scores are typically in the
0.05-0.25 range, with the highest we observed being 0.254 (calm). For
comparison, random vectors in d=2304 have expected |cosine| ≈ 0.021.
Our signals are 3-12x above random, which is statistically significant
but means the emotion directions explain a very small fraction of the
residual stream's total variance. The model's emotional processing is a
minor component of what the residual stream encodes.

## 7. Visualization

**The orb is an interpretation, not raw data.** The mapping from emotion
scores to visual properties (color, size, motion, complexity) involves
multiple design choices:
- Which emotions map to which colors (OKLCH palette)
- How valence/arousal metadata weights the blend
- Spring constant tuning for transition speed
- Intensity thresholds for particle emission
These are aesthetic/perceptual decisions that affect how the data "reads."
Different visualization choices could produce different intuitive impressions
from the same underlying data.

**The demo runs on a 2.6B model with ~1-2 minute generation time.** This is
not representative of production LLM behavior. A frontier-scale model would
have different emotion dynamics, faster inference, and potentially the
thermostat regulation that our model lacks.

## 8. Reproducibility

**TransformerLens version sensitivity.** TL 2.x applies LayerNorm folding
and writing-weight centering that change raw activation values (but not
directions, since we L2-normalize). Results may differ slightly with
different TL versions or with direct HuggingFace hook access.

**Gemma model version.** We use google/gemma-2-2b-it from HuggingFace.
If Google updates the model weights under the same name (which has happened
with other models), results would not reproduce. We should pin the model
revision hash.

**Non-deterministic elements.** While the extraction pipeline is deterministic
given the same inputs, the model loading and GPU computation may produce
slightly different floating-point results across different hardware or
CUDA versions.

**Probe layer default bug (discovered and fixed).** `models.py` hardcoded
`int(n_layers * 2/3)` for the probe layer default, giving layer 17 for
Gemma 2 2B. The validated optimal layer is 22. All speaker separation
runs v1 through v3 extracted at the wrong layer. Fixed to
`round(n_layers * config.probe_layer_fraction)` where the config default
is `22/26`. The v4 speaker separation (layer 22) is the first correct run.

## 9. Methodology Caveats for Cross-Model Application

**The pipeline was validated on one model.** Every methodological choice
(probe layer fraction, content-token detection, response-prep probing)
was validated on Gemma 2 2B IT. When applying to other models:
- The optimal probe layer fraction (0.846) may differ. Always run a sweep.
- The `find_content_token_range` function has anchor detection for Gemma
  and Llama chat templates but is untested on Mistral, Phi, Qwen, etc.
- The response-prep position advantage (83% vs 75%) may not replicate
  on models with different chat template structures.
- TransformerLens support varies by model family. Gemma 2 works well;
  other models may require the HuggingFace fallback backend.
